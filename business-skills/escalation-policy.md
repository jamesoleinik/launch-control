# Escalation Policy

## Description
Follow this policy when a launch task or milestone is blocked. Determines severity, notification chain, and resolution timeline.

## Instructions

When a task or milestone is blocked, follow this escalation policy to resolve the blocker.

### Step 1: Assess Severity
- **Low:** Task is blocked but milestone due date is >5 days away. Owner can resolve independently.
- **Medium:** Task is blocked and milestone due date is 2-5 days away. Requires team lead notification.
- **High:** Task is blocked and milestone due date is <2 days away, OR multiple tasks in the same milestone are blocked. Requires PM and leadership notification.
- **Critical:** A blocked task is on the critical path and the launch date is at risk. Requires immediate escalation to the launch sponsor.

### Step 2: Notify
- **Low:** Send a reminder to the task owner with the blocker reason and a request for an ETA.
- **Medium:** Notify the task owner AND the milestone owner. Request a resolution plan within 24 hours.
- **High:** Notify the task owner, milestone owner, AND the program manager. Schedule a 15-min sync within 4 hours.
- **Critical:** Notify all of the above PLUS the launch sponsor. Schedule an emergency sync within 1 hour.

### Step 3: Track Resolution
- Create a status update on the launch record documenting: the blocker, severity, who was notified, and the expected resolution date.
- Update the task's blocker reason field with the current status.
- If the blocker is not resolved within the SLA (Low: 3 days, Medium: 24 hours, High: 4 hours, Critical: 1 hour), automatically re-escalate to the next severity level.

### Step 4: Post-Resolution
- Update the task status to "In Progress" or "Done" once the blocker is resolved.
- Add a status update summarizing the resolution.
- If the blocker caused a milestone to slip, update the milestone due date and notify the program manager.
